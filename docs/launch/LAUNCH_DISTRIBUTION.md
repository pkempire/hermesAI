# Hermes Launch Distribution

## Launch Goal

Optimize for 20 real users who run one workflow and reach one reviewed email
draft. Do not optimize for vanity signups first.

The activation event:

> User creates or approves one Gmail draft from a Hermes-generated prospect
> card with evidence attached.

## Best Initial Audience

Prioritize people who already feel the pain:

- founder-led B2B startups doing their own outbound
- GTM engineers and RevOps builders
- Clay/Apify/n8n/Zapier power users
- agencies building lead lists for clients
- local-services marketers
- field marketing and event marketers
- recruiters sourcing niche profiles

## Where To Launch

### Day 0: Warm Operator Launch

Channels:

- X/Twitter founder and GTM circles
- LinkedIn post from founder account
- direct DMs to 30 to 50 operators
- SF founder/operator group chats
- Clay, Apify, n8n, RevOps, and GTM engineering communities where allowed
- small private Loom to specific users, not generic blast

Ask:

- "Bring me one campaign you have been putting off."
- "I will help you get to one reviewed draft."
- "Tell me where the workflow breaks."

Give:

- one-month code
- no-card trial
- request for one concrete feedback note or screenshot

### Day 2-7: Public Build-In-Public

Post short demos by workflow, not generic product tours:

- local services review pain
- conference sponsor follow-up
- hiring-signal outbound
- ecommerce marketplace research
- partner directory scraping
- competitor review conquest
- recruiting target map

Each post should show:

1. prompt
2. source plan
3. prospect card with evidence
4. draft
5. Gmail draft or MCP call

### After 5-10 Strong Workflows: Product Hunt

Launch on Product Hunt only after the activation loop is stable. The Product
Hunt story should be founder-built and concrete:

- "I kept not sending outbound, so I built a GTM engineer that gets me to one
  good draft."
- show one practical workflow in the first GIF
- include a free/no-card trial
- ask for weird workflows in the comments

### Open-Source Angle

Use this once the repo has clean setup docs:

- Show HN: Hermes, an open-source AI GTM engineer for reviewable outbound
- r/SideProject
- r/SaaS
- r/sales only if framed as workflow tooling, not spam automation
- Indie Hackers
- relevant Discord/Slack groups where open-source tools are welcome

Do not lead with "AI SDR." Lead with:

> open-source GTM workflow engine with live source discovery, enrichment,
> evidence cards, Gmail drafts, and MCP.

## Competitor Notes: Origami

Origami's public positioning is strong:

- one-prompt prospecting
- live sources instead of static databases
- signal-qualified lists
- verified email and phone waterfalls
- built-in sequencing
- broad examples across healthcare, fintech, ecommerce, biotech, proptech,
  hospitality, logistics, manufacturing, and nonprofits

Hermes should not try to out-claim them before the backend supports it.

Hermes can differentiate with:

- open-source / self-hostable trust
- MCP-first power-user workflows
- review-first cards and evidence
- deterministic workflow runs instead of black-box agent output
- Gmail-first drafts for founder-led motion
- templates that compile into inspectable workflows
- eventually, custom source capabilities users can add

## Launch Assets To Record

Record these as separate 15 to 45 second clips:

- Prompt to plan: messy prompt becomes a workflow.
- Evidence card: show why one account matched.
- Draft from evidence: one email with source-backed personalization.
- MCP call: external agent starts a deterministic workflow.
- Template marketplace: pick a workflow and run.

## Demo Workflow Menu

Use broad markets, not only AI startups.

### Local Services Review Pain

Prompt:

```text
Find 25 commercial roofing companies in Chicago with recent Google reviews
mentioning leaks, slow response, warranty issues, or poor follow-up. Find the
owner or operations lead and draft a respectful email for review monitoring
software.
```

Why it works:

- easy to understand
- large TAM
- clear evidence
- shows why live web beats Apollo

### Conference Sponsor Follow-Up

Prompt:

```text
Find 40 companies sponsoring supply chain or manufacturing conferences in the
US this quarter. Identify the marketing or events lead and draft a post-event
content production pitch using the conference context.
```

Why it works:

- real GTM operator workflow
- clear time-sensitive signal
- useful for agencies and B2B services

### Hiring Signal Outbound

Prompt:

```text
Find 30 companies hiring field service dispatchers or operations managers in
Texas. Identify the VP Ops or owner and draft an email for workforce scheduling
software.
```

Why it works:

- broad TAM
- buying signal is obvious
- no need to explain AI-startup jargon

### Partner Marketplace Finder

Prompt:

```text
Find 50 accounting firms that serve construction companies and publish advisory
or bookkeeping services. Identify the partner or growth lead and draft a
co-marketing partnership email.
```

Why it works:

- partnership motion is less spammy
- shows websites/directories/source extraction
- usable by many service businesses

## Copy Rules

Say:

- "reviewed Gmail draft"
- "live evidence"
- "source-backed prospect cards"
- "deterministic workflow"
- "MCP for power users"
- "no-card trial"

Avoid:

- guaranteed pipeline
- fully autonomous sending
- meta copy about the claim itself
- vague "AI copilot"
- "saves 8 hours" unless measured inside Hermes
- inflated response rates
